## H2: Step-by-Step: How to Import Your Pet to Dubai

### Step 1: Check Your Breed and Country (Day 0)

Before you do anything, confirm two things:

1. **Is your dog's breed banned in the UAE?** (See the banned breeds section below.) If your dog is on the list, you cannot import it — unless it is a certified service animal.
2. **Is your origin country high-risk or low-risk for rabies?** This determines whether you need a titer test and how long you'll wait.

> 🚩 **Flagged uncertainty:** The exact high-risk/low-risk country list changes. Always verify your country's status on the [MOCCAE portal](https://www.moccae.gov.ae/en/services/export-import-services/import-permit-pets.aspx) before starting.

### Step 2: Implant the Microchip (Day 0)

Your pet must have an **ISO 11784/11785 compliant 15-digit microchip**. This must be implanted **before** the rabies vaccination. If your pet was vaccinated before being microchipped, the vaccination is invalid for UAE entry.

- Cost: AED 300–600 at your local vet
- The microchip number must appear on every subsequent document

### Step 3: Rabies Vaccination (Day 0)

Your pet must be at least **12 weeks old** at the time of vaccination. The vaccine must be:

- Inactivated or recombinant (**not** attenuated live virus)
- Administered **≥21 days before travel**
- Within **12 months of arrival** (or a valid booster documented)

### Step 4: Core Vaccinations (Day 0–14)

**Dogs:** DHPPIL (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza, Leptospirosis)
**Cats:** FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia)

These are typically administered alongside the rabies vaccine or shortly after.

### Step 5: Rabies Titer Test — If Required (Day 21–31)

If your pet is from a **high-risk country**, a blood sample must be drawn **≥21 days after** the rabies vaccination. The sample is sent to a **WOAH/ISO 17025 accredited laboratory**.

- Minimum result: **≥0.5 IU/ml**
- You must then wait **90 days from the date of blood sample collection** before your pet can enter the UAE

> ⚠️ **Special exception:** Pets from **Bahrain and Qatar** must have a titer test **even though they are classified as low-risk**. This is frequently missed and causes delays.

### Step 6: Apply for MOCCAE Import Permit (Day 21–90, depending on country)

Apply online via the [MOCCAE portal](https://www.moccae.gov.ae) or the MOCCAE mobile app.

- Fee: **~AED 200 per animal**
- Validity: **30–90 days** from issue (some sources say 30 days, others 90 for UK/EU origins — verify in the portal at the time of application.)
- Processing time: **3–7 working days**
- Required: Copy of owner's passport, UAE visa, pet's microchip number, vaccination records, and titer test result (if applicable)

> 🚩 **Flagged uncertainty:** Import permit validity has changed multiple times. Apply as close to your travel date as safely possible while leaving time for flight booking.

### Step 7: Book Manifest Cargo Flight (2–4 weeks before travel)

All pets entering Dubai **must travel as manifest cargo**. They cannot fly in the cabin or as checked baggage on flights **to** Dubai.

- **Emirates SkyCargo:** Most common for DXB arrivals. 72 hours advance notice required.
- **Etihad Cargo:** Alternative for Abu Dhabi arrivals. Etihad also allows small pets in cabin **to Abu Dhabi only** (≤8 kg, $399 per segment).
- **flydubai Cargo:** Limited published pet policies — contact directly.

Cargo costs typically range from **AED 3,000–12,000** depending on route, airline, and pet size.

### Step 8: Health Certificate and Antiparasitics (Within 10–14 days of travel)

- **Antiparasitic treatment:** Internal (deworming/Praziquantel) and external (Fipronil or Permethrin) within **14 days before arrival**
- **International Health Certificate:** Issued by a government-accredited veterinarian within **10 days of arrival**

### Step 9: Arrival and Customs Clearance (Day of arrival)

Your pet arrives at DXB or DWC cargo terminal. You (or your agent) present:

- MOCCAE import permit
- Original health certificate
- Vaccination records
- Microchip certificate
- Titer test result (if applicable)
- Antiparasitic treatment record
- Owner's passport and UAE visa

If all documents are correct, customs clearance takes **2–4 hours**. Your pet does not enter quarantine.

### Step 10: Dubai Municipality Registration (Within 30 days)

Register your pet via the **Aleef app** or Dubai Smart Services portal. You need:

- Your Emirates ID
- Pet's microchip number
- Current vaccination records
- Pet photo
- Your residence address (Makani number)

- Fee: ~AED 10
- Annual renewal required

A physical municipality ID tag must be worn on your pet's collar at all times.

These breeds are permitted in the UAE but have **additional restrictions in Dubai**:

## H2: Vaccination Requirements

### H3: Rabies Vaccination (Mandatory for All)

- **Minimum age:** 12 weeks at first vaccination
- **Pre-travel window:** Administered between 21 days and 12 months prior to entering the UAE
- **Accepted types:** Inactivated or recombinant vaccines only
- **Not accepted:** Attenuated live virus vaccines
- **Boosters:** Annual booster required, or follow manufacturer's guidance for 2-3 year vaccines

### H3: Core Vaccinations — Dogs

- Canine Distemper Virus (CDV)
- Canine Parvovirus (CPV)
- Infectious Canine Hepatitis (Adenovirus)
- Leptospirosis (Canicola and Icterohaemorrhagiae)

### H3: Core Vaccinations — Cats

- Feline Panleukopenia (FPV / Feline Distemper)
- Feline Rhinotracheitis (FHV-1 / Herpes Virus)
- Feline Calicivirus (FCV)
- Feline Leukemia (FeLV) — recommended by some authorities

### H3: Vaccination Timing — Why Order Matters

1. **Microchip first.** Always. The microchip must precede the rabies vaccination.
2. **Rabies vaccination second.** Wait ≥21 days before travel.
3. **Core vaccines third.** Can be given alongside rabies or shortly after.
4. **Titer test fourth (if required).** Blood drawn ≥21 days after rabies vaccination.
5. **Health certificate last.** Within 10 days of arrival.

> ⚠️ **Warning:** If your vaccination records show the rabies vaccine was given before the microchip, MOCCAE will reject your import permit. You'll need to re-vaccinate after microchipping and restart the timeline.

## H2: Rabies Titer Test Rules

The rabies titer test (also called RNATT — Rabies Neutralising Antibody Titre Test) proves that your pet's rabies vaccine worked.

### H3: Do You Need a Titer Test?

**Yes, if your pet is from a high-risk country.** Most of Africa, Latin America, Central Asia, the Middle East, and parts of Asia are classified as high-risk.

**No, if your pet is from a low-risk country:** UK, EU, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Singapore, and most other developed nations.

**Yes, even if low-risk:** Pets from **Bahrain and Qatar** must have a titer test. This is a specific exception.

> 🚩 **Flagged uncertainty:** The high-risk/low-risk country list is maintained by MOCCAE and changes. Verify your country's current status in the MOCCAE portal before skipping the titer test.

### H3: Titer Test Requirements

- Blood sample drawn **≥21 days after** primary rabies vaccination
- Minimum result: **≥0.5 IU/ml**
- Must be processed at a **WOAH/ISO 17025 accredited laboratory**
- **90-day waiting period** from the date of blood sample collection before the pet can enter the UAE
- The test result must be included in your MOCCAE permit application

### H3: Titer Test Timeline Example

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Microchip + rabies vaccination |
| Day 21 | Blood sample for titer test |
| Day 31 | Receive result (≥0.5 IU/ml) |
| Day 111 | Apply for MOCCAE import permit (90-day wait complete) |
| Day 118 | Book cargo flight |
| Day 121 | Antiparasitic treatment + health certificate |
| Day 121+ | Pet arrives in Dubai |

**Total minimum time from scratch for high-risk countries: ~4 months.**

> ⚠️ **Warning:** The 90-day waiting period is **not negotiable**. You cannot apply for the import permit or book travel until this period has passed. There are no exceptions.

## H2: Airline-Specific Requirements

All pets entering Dubai **must travel as manifest cargo**. They cannot fly in the cabin or as checked baggage on flights to Dubai — with one exception.

### H3: Emirates SkyCargo

Emirates is the most common airline for pet transport to Dubai.

- **In-cabin:** No regular pets allowed. Only trained service dogs and falcons on specific routes.
- **Checked baggage:** Not accepted for flights to Dubai.
- **Cargo (SkyCargo):** Mandatory for most pets. Cost: typically $1,500–$5,000 USD depending on route and size.
- **Advance notice:** Minimum 72 hours
- **Required documents:** Health certificate, import permit, photos of pet in crate, Owner Acknowledgement Form
- **Crate:** IATA-approved, rigid, leak-proof. For restricted breeds, crate must be at least 10% larger than standard.
- **Banned breeds:** Even stricter than UAE law. Includes snub-nosed breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs, Shih Tzus) with seasonal restrictions (November–April only).
- **Transit care:** Pets in transit >6 hours in Dubai are cared for at the Emirates Pet Lounge.

### H3: Etihad Airways

Etihad is the most pet-friendly UAE airline — but only for Abu Dhabi.

- **In-cabin (to Abu Dhabi only):** Small dogs and cats allowed. Pet + carrier must weigh ≤8 kg. Carrier max: 40 x 40 x 22 cm (Economy). Fee: USD 399 per flight segment.
- **Checked baggage:** Accepted on some routes depending on destination rules.
- **Cargo:** Available for larger pets or routes where cabin/checked is not permitted.
- **Advance notice:** Book via Contact Centre; submit form 7 days before, documents 72 hours before.
- **Banned breeds:** Same as UAE federal list, plus Boxer.
- **Abu Dhabi entry:** Requires a Bill of Entry from MICCO Logistics (cost: AED 365) at least 24 hours before departure.
- **Pet age:** Must be ≥16 weeks old for in-cabin.

### H3: flydubai

- flydubai does not have a published pet-in-cabin policy.
- Pets are generally handled as cargo via flydubai's cargo services or partners.
- **Recommendation:** Contact flydubai Cargo directly for arrangements.

### H3: Airline Comparison Summary

## H2: Health Certificate Timing

The International Health Certificate is the last document you obtain before travel. Get the timing wrong, and it expires before your pet arrives.

- **Valid for 10 days** from the date of issuance to the date of arrival in the UAE
- Must be issued by a **government-accredited or official veterinarian**
- Must include antiparasitic treatment records
- Must show the same microchip number as all other documents

### H3: When to Schedule the Health Certificate

- **Book the vet appointment 2–3 days before your pet's flight**
- The certificate must be issued within 10 days of arrival, so work backwards from your arrival date
- If your flight is delayed and the certificate expires, you must obtain a new one

> 🚩 **Flagged uncertainty:** Some countries require additional government endorsement (e.g., DEFRA in the UK, USDA in the USA, CFIA in Canada). This can add 2–5 days. Confirm your origin country's endorsement requirements with your vet.

## H2: What Happens If Your Documents Are Wrong

This is the section most pet owners skip — and the one that causes the most pain.

### H3: No Quarantine (If Documents Are Correct)

If all your paperwork is complete, correct, and consistent, your pet **does not need to enter quarantine**. This is one of the UAE's biggest advantages as a pet relocation destination.

### H3: Conditional Quarantine (If Documents Are Wrong)

Quarantine is imposed if:
- Your pet arrives from a **high-risk country without a titer test**
- Documentation is incomplete, incorrect, or non-sequential (e.g., vaccination before microchip)
- The pet shows clinical signs of illness on arrival
- Breed identification is disputed by the inspector

**Quarantine details:**
- Location: Dubai Cargo Village or Abu Dhabi Cargo Terminal
- Cost: **Entirely borne by the owner**
- Duration: Until the issue is resolved (typically 30 days for titer testing)
- One reported case cost **AED 8,500** for a 30-day quarantine after a missing titer test

### H3: Return or Euthanasia (Worst Cases)

In extreme cases:
- The animal may be **returned to the origin country on the next available flight** at the owner's expense
- In the worst cases, **euthanasia** may be ordered if the animal poses a rabies risk and cannot be returned

> ⚠️ **Critical Warning:** These are not theoretical risks. They happen when pet owners assume "it will be fine" or rely on outdated information. We check every document three times because we've seen what happens when one date is wrong.

### H3: The Most Common Document Errors

1. **Microchip before vaccination** — Vaccination given before microchip = invalid
2. **Titer test timing** — Blood drawn before 21 days post-vaccination = invalid
3. **Permit validity** — Permit expired before travel = entry denied
4. **Health certificate expiry** — Certificate older than 10 days on arrival = quarantine risk
5. **Mismatched microchip numbers** — Different numbers on different documents = automatic rejection

## H2: Frequently Asked Questions

### H3: Do dogs need to be quarantined in Dubai?

**No.** If all documentation is complete and correct — including the MOCCAE import permit, ISO microchip, rabies vaccination, and health certificate — pets do not require mandatory quarantine in Dubai. However, incomplete documentation can result in conditional quarantine at the owner's expense.

### H3: What is the MOCCAE import permit for pets?

The MOCCAE import permit is a mandatory approval from the UAE Ministry of Climate Change and Environment required for all pets entering the UAE. It costs approximately AED 200 per animal and is valid for 30–90 days depending on the origin country. You must apply online via the MOCCAE portal or app before your pet travels.

### H3: Is a rabies titer test required for Dubai?

A rabies titer test is only required for pets coming from **high-risk rabies countries**. It is **not required** for pets from low-risk countries including the UK, EU, USA, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand. However, pets from **Bahrain and Qatar** are a special exception — they do require a titer test despite being classified as low-risk.

### H3: What documents are needed to bring a cat to the UAE?

To bring a cat to the UAE, you need: (1) MOCCAE import permit, (2) ISO 11784/11785 microchip certificate, (3) rabies vaccination certificate (≥21 days old), (4) FVRCP vaccination records, (5) international health certificate (within 10 days of travel), (6) antiparasitic treatment record, and (7) copy of owner's passport and UAE visa.

### H3: How long does pet relocation to Dubai take?

From low-risk countries like the UK or EU, the process takes **4–6 weeks**. From high-risk countries requiring a rabies titer test, the minimum timeline is **4 months** (including the 90-day waiting period after the titer test). We recommend starting the process at least **8 weeks** before your planned move.
