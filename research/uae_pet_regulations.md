## 1. UAE Federal Laws & Governing Authority

- **Primary Authority:** The **Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (MOCCAE)** controls all animal imports into the UAE. No pet may enter without MOCCAE approval [^1][^2].
- **Key Legislation:**
  - **Federal Law No. 8 of 2013** — Concerning the Prevention and Control of Animal Infectious Diseases. This is the federal legal framework for health control [^3].
  - **Ministerial Resolution No. 448 of 2017** — Regulating the Importation of Cats and Dogs. Establishes microchip, vaccination, rabies titre test, antiparasitic treatment, banned breed list, and 90-day waiting period requirements [^3].
- **Allowed Animals:** Only **domestic dogs and cats** are permitted for standard import. Exotic animals are generally prohibited. Birds require a separate MOCCAE import permit and may be subject to CITES requirements [^1][^2].
- **Wolf hybrids, Savannah cats, and Bengal cats** are not permitted unless 5th generation removed from the pedigree [^1].
- **Pet Limit:** Maximum **2 pets per individual per year** (two cats, two dogs, or one of each). A licensed agent can sometimes arrange to increase this to five [^1][^4].
- **Minimum Age:** Pets must be **at least 4 months old** (some sources say 3 months for vaccination, but 4 months for entry) [^2][^5].

## 2. Required Documentation

All documentation must be consistent — the **microchip number** must appear on every certificate, and all dates must align sequentially.

### 2.1 Core Documents (Import)

| Document | Details | Validity |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **MOCCAE Import Permit** | Applied online via moccae.gov.ae or the MOCCAE app. Fee: ~AED 200 per animal. | **30–90 days** from issue (see §10 for conflicting guidance) [^2][^6] |
| **ISO Microchip Certificate** | 15-digit ISO 11784/11785 compliant. Must be implanted **before** rabies vaccination. | Permanent [^1][^3] |
| **Rabies Vaccination Certificate** | Administered at ≥12 weeks of age; ≥21 days before travel; within 12 months of arrival. | 1 year (annual booster required) [^1][^3] |
| **Core Vaccination Records** | Dogs: DHPPIL / DHLPP. Cats: FVRCP / Tricat. | Per vaccine schedule [^1][^7] |
| **Rabies Titer Test (RNATT)** | Required for **high-risk countries** only. Blood sample ≥21 days after rabies vaccination. Minimum threshold: ≥0.5 IU/ml. | 90-day wait from sample collection for high-risk countries [^1][^3] |
| **International Health Certificate** | Issued by government-accredited / official veterinarian in origin country. Must include parasite treatments. | **10 days** from issuance to arrival [^3][^8] |
| **Antiparasitic Treatment Record** | Internal (deworming / Praziquantel) and external (Fipronil or Permethrin) within **14 days** before arrival [^3][^7]. | — |
| **Copy of Owner's Passport & UAE Visa** | Required for permit application and customs clearance. | — |

### 2.2 Document Conflicts & Uncertainties

> **🚩 Flagged Uncertainty:**
> - **Import Permit Validity:** Older guidance (2023–2024) consistently states 30 days [^1][^8]. Some 2026 sources now state **90 days** for permits issued from the UK [^9]. **Recommendation:** Apply for the permit as close to travel as safely possible, and confirm the exact validity window in the MOCCAE portal at the time of application.
> - **Minimum Age for Entry:** Some sources state 3 months (for vaccination), while others state 4 months for actual entry. The 2023 Elsenburg infosheet explicitly says "Pets under 4 months are not allowed" [^10]. **Recommendation:** Plan for the pet to be at least 4 months old at arrival.
> - **Titer Test for Bahrain & Qatar:** Despite being classified as low-risk, pets from Bahrain and Qatar **must** have a rabies titer test [^1]. This is a specific exception that is frequently missed.

## 3. Banned & Restricted Dog Breeds

### 3.1 Completely Banned (Cannot Be Imported or Owned)

The UAE enforces a strict **federal breed ban** under Ministerial Resolution No. 448 / 2017. The following breeds and their mixes/crosses **cannot be imported**:

- **Pit Bull Types:**
  - American Pit Bull Terrier
  - Staffordshire Bull Terrier
  - American Staffordshire Terrier
  - American Bully
  - Any Bull Terrier or Pit Bull cross
- **Mastiff Types:**
  - Brazilian Mastiff (Fila Brasileiro)
  - Argentinian Mastiff (Dogo Argentino)
  - Tibetan Mastiff
  - Neapolitan Mastiff
  - French Mastiff (Dogue de Bordeaux)
  - Boerboel
  - Bullmastiff
  - Cane Corso (Italian Mastiff)
  - Bully Kutta (Alangu / Indian Mastiff)
  - Perro de Presa Canario (Canary Mastiff)
  - Any Mastiff or hybrid
- **Japanese Tosa (Tosa Inu)**
- **Presa Canario**
- **Wolf-dog hybrids** (any dog mixed with a wolf)

[^1][^4][^10][^11]

> **🚩 Flagged Uncertainty:** Some sources list **Rottweiler, Doberman Pinscher, and Boxer** as fully banned [^2][^12]. Other authoritative sources (including 2026 pet travel guides) classify these as **restricted** rather than fully banned — meaning they may be permitted with special permits, muzzling, and housing restrictions [^4][^11]. **Always verify with MOCCAE before importing these breeds.**

### 3.2 Restricted Breeds (Dubai-Specific Rules)

In Dubai, the following breeds (in addition to the banned list above) have **additional restrictions** — they **cannot live in apartments**, must be **leashed and muzzled** in public, and must be registered with the Veterinary Services Division:

- Bull Terrier (including Miniature Bull Terrier)
- Husky
- English Bulldog / Old English Bulldog
- Perro de Presa Mallorquin
- Shar Pei
- Rottweiler (in some community contexts)
- Doberman Pinscher (in some community contexts)

[^1][^13]

### 3.3 Service / Emotional Support Animal Exceptions

Banned breeds may receive an exception **only** if they are certified as:
- **Service animals** (trained by an ADI or IGDF accredited organization)
- **Emotional support animals** (for medical purposes)

To qualify, the owner must provide:
- A training record from an accredited training centre
- Suitable medical records justifying the need for the animal

[^4][^14]

> **Penalties for non-compliance:** Fines ranging from **AED 10,000 to AED 700,000**, plus possible jail sentences and **confiscation of the animal** [^4].

## 4. Vaccination Requirements

### 4.1 Rabies Vaccination

- **Mandatory:** Yes, for all dogs and cats over 3 months of age.
- **Minimum Age:** 12 weeks (3 months) at first vaccination.
- **Pre-Travel Window:** Must be administered **between 21 days and 12 months** prior to entering the UAE [^1][^3].
- **Accepted Types:** Inactivated or recombinant vaccines only. **Attenuated live virus vaccines are NOT accepted** [^3].
- **Boosters:** If using a 2- or 3-year vaccine, MOCCAE requires that the annual booster be documented or that the vaccine complies with the manufacturer's current regulation [^3].

### 4.2 Core Vaccinations — Dogs

- Canine Distemper Virus (CDV)
- Canine Parvovirus (CPV)
- Infectious Canine Hepatitis (Adenovirus)
- Leptospirosis (Canicola and Icterohaemorrhagiae)

[^1][^7][^10]

### 4.3 Core Vaccinations — Cats

- Feline Panleukopenia (FPV / Feline Distemper)
- Feline Rhinotracheitis (FHV-1 / Herpes Virus)
- Feline Calicivirus (FCV)
- Feline Leukemia (FeLV) — recommended by some authorities [^1][^7]

### 4.4 Rabies Titer Test (RNATT / Serology)

- **Required for:** Pets from **high-risk rabies countries** (most of Africa, Latin America, Central Asia, Middle East, and parts of Asia) [^3][^8].
- **Not required for:** Pets from **low-risk countries** (UK, EU, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Singapore, etc.) [^3][^9].
- **Minimum Threshold:** ≥0.5 IU/ml [^10].
- **Sample Timing:** Blood drawn **≥21 days after** primary rabies vaccination [^3].
- **Waiting Period:** **90 days (3 months)** from the date of blood sample collection before the pet can enter the UAE [^3][^8].
- **Accredited Labs:** Must be processed at a WOAH/ISO 17025 accredited laboratory [^3].
- **Special Note:** Pets from **Bahrain and Qatar must have a titer test** even though they are low-risk [^1].

> **🚩 Flagged Uncertainty:** Some older sources mention a 30-day wait after vaccination before the titer test (not 21 days). The 2026 MOCCAE Import Guide and Zoovet Travel confirm 21 days [^3]. Always follow the most current MOCCAE guidance.

## 5. Quarantine Rules

### 5.1 Standard Import (No Quarantine)

- **If all documentation is in order**, pets **do NOT require mandatory quarantine** in the UAE [^1][^2][^6].
- This is one of the UAE's main advantages as a pet relocation destination — there is no mandatory government quarantine facility for compliant pets.

### 5.2 Conditional Quarantine

Quarantine **is imposed** if:
- The pet arrives from a **high-risk country without a rabies titer test** — the pet will be quarantined and a titer test performed at the **owner's expense** [^1][^6].
- Documentation is incomplete, incorrect, or non-sequential (e.g., vaccination before microchip).
- The pet shows clinical signs of illness on arrival.
- Breed identification is disputed by the inspector.

**Quarantine facilities:** Dubai Cargo Village / Abu Dhabi Cargo Terminal [^3].

**Cost:** Entirely borne by the owner. One reported case cost **AED 8,500** for a 30-day quarantine after a missing titer test [^6].

> **🚩 Warning:** Incomplete documentation can result in the animal being **returned to the origin country on the next available flight** or, in the worst cases, **euthanasia** [^6].

## 6. Airline-Specific Policies

### 6.1 General Rule: Manifest Cargo Only

All pets entering the UAE **must travel as manifest (air) cargo**. They **cannot** travel as checked baggage or in the cabin on flights **to** Dubai — **with one exception** (see Etihad below) [^1][^2][^6].

### 6.2 Emirates

- **In-cabin:** No regular pets allowed. Only **trained service dogs** and **falcons** on specific routes [^15][^16].
- **Checked Baggage:** Generally **not accepted** for flights to Dubai. Pets must travel via **Emirates SkyCargo** [^15][^16].
- **SkyCargo:** Mandatory for most pets. Charges typically **$1,500–$5,000** depending on route and size [^15].
- **Banned Breeds (Emirates-specific):** Even stricter than UAE law. Includes snub-nosed breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs, Shih Tzus, etc.) which have seasonal restrictions (Nov–Apr only) [^16].
- **Booking:** Minimum 72 hours advance notice. Requires photos of pet in crate, health certificates, and an Owner Acknowledgement Form [^16].
- **Crate:** IATA-approved, rigid, leak-proof. For restricted breeds, crate must be at least 10% larger than standard [^15].
- **Transit Care:** Pets in transit >6 hours in Dubai are cared for at the Pet Lounge [^15].

### 6.3 Etihad Airways

- **In-cabin (PETC):** Etihad is the **only UAE airline** that allows small dogs and cats in the cabin on flights **to/from Abu Dhabi** (NOT Dubai). [^17][^18]
  - Pet + carrier must weigh **≤8 kg**.
  - Carrier max size: 40 x 40 x 22 cm (Economy) or 50 x 43 x 50 cm (Business with extra seat) [^17].
  - Pet must be **≥16 weeks old**.
  - Fee: **USD 399** per flight segment (Economy); Business requires extra seat + USD 399 [^17][^18].
  - Must be booked via Contact Centre; submit form **7 days** before, documents **72 hours** before [^17].
- **Checked Baggage (AVIH):** Accepted on some routes depending on destination country rules [^17].
- **Cargo:** For larger pets or routes where cabin/checked is not permitted [^17].
- **Banned Breeds:** Same as UAE federal list, plus Boxer [^17].
- **Abu Dhabi Entry:** Requires a **Bill of Entry** from MICCO Logistics (cost: AED 365) at least 24 hours before departure [^17].

### 6.4 flydubai

- flydubai does **not** have a published pet-in-cabin policy comparable to Etihad.
- Pets are generally handled as **cargo** via flydubai's cargo services or partners [^19].
- **Recommendation:** Contact flydubai Cargo directly for pet transport arrangements.

### 6.5 Summary Comparison

| Airline | Cabin to UAE? | Checked Baggage to UAE? | Cargo to UAE? | Notes |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|---------------|-------|
| **Emirates** | ❌ No (except service dogs) | ❌ No (to Dubai) | ✅ Yes (SkyCargo) | Most common for DXB arrivals; strict breed list |
| **Etihad** | ✅ Yes (to Abu Dhabi only, ≤8kg) | ✅ Some routes | ✅ Yes | Most pet-friendly UAE airline; EY only |
| **flydubai** | ❌ No | ❌ No | ✅ Yes (Cargo) | Limited published pet policies |

## 8. Process Timeline

### 8.1 Minimum Timeline — From Scratch (High-Risk Country)

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| **Day 0** | ISO microchip implantation + rabies primary vaccination + core vaccines. Pet must be ≥12 weeks old. |
| **Day 21** | Earliest date for rabies titer test blood sample (RNATT) |
| **Day 31–36** | Receive positive RNATT result (≥0.5 IU/ml) |
| **Day 96** | Apply for MOCCAE Import Permit (90 days from sample collection elapsed) |
| **Day 107–111** | Administer antiparasitic treatments (within 14 days of arrival) |
| **Day 112** | Earliest suitable date for shipment to UAE |
| **Day 112–122** | Obtain International Health Certificate (within 10 days of arrival) |
| **Day 112+** | Pet arrives at DXB/DWC/AUH/SHJ as manifest cargo. Customs clearance and collection. |

**Minimum total from scratch: ~4 months** [^3][^8]

### 8.2 Minimum Timeline — From Scratch (Low-Risk Country, e.g., UK, EU)

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| **Day 0** | ISO microchip + rabies vaccination + core vaccines |
| **Day 21** | Earliest travel date (rabies 21-day wait complete) |
| **Day 21–30** | Apply for MOCCAE Import Permit; book cargo flight |
| **Day 28–30** | Antiparasitic treatments; health certificate issued |
| **Day 30** | Travel to UAE |

**Minimum total from scratch: ~4–6 weeks** (assuming no delays) [^9]

### 8.3 Post-Arrival Steps (Dubai)

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Within 30 days** | Register pet with Dubai Municipality (mandatory) [^13] |
| **Annual** | Renew vaccinations and municipality registration |
| **Ongoing** | Dogs must be leashed and muzzled in public; ID tag worn at all times [^13] |

## 9. Export from Dubai / UAE

### 9.1 Basic Requirements

To export a pet **from** the UAE, you need:

1. **ISO-compliant microchip** (15-digit, ISO 11784/11785) — must precede rabies vaccination [^20]
2. **Valid rabies vaccination** — between 30 days and 12 months prior to export [^1][^20]
3. **Pet passport / vaccination book** — complete history with microchip number, batch numbers, manufacturer details [^20]
4. **Fit to Fly Certificate** — issued by your vet within 5–10 days of travel (validity varies by destination) [^20]
5. **MOCCAE Export Health Certificate** — mandatory for **all** pets leaving UAE. Applied for online; valid **30 days**. Requires in-person inspection at DXB or AUH Cargo Village with a MOCCAE vet [^20]
6. **Travel Certificate** (for EU, UK, etc.) — issued by your vet and endorsed by MOCCAE [^20]
7. **IATA-approved travel crate**
8. **Rabies Titer Test** — if required by destination country (e.g., Australia, New Zealand, Japan). Not required for UK/EU/USA if vaccination is current [^20]

### 9.2 No UAE Exit Quarantine

There is **no quarantine requirement for pets departing the UAE**. Quarantine rules are set by the **destination country**, not the UAE [^20].

### 9.3 Returning to the UAE

If you plan to return to the UAE after leaving:
- Your pet must have a **microchip**, **current rabies vaccination**, **health certificate**, and **export permit** on departure.
- On return, a **Re-Entry card** is needed instead of a new import permit.
- No rabies titer test is required on return **as long as the rabies vaccination has not expired** [^1][^20].

### 9.4 Driving to Oman / Border Warning

> **🚩 Critical Warning:** Do **not** attempt to drive to Oman without proper documentation. Oman requires a **Rabies Titer Test** (2–3 week processing). Additionally, travelling onwards from Oman to most countries requires a **90-day waiting period** from the date of the test. There are confirmed reports of pets being **abandoned at the Oman border** after owners were refused entry [^20].

## 10. Recent Regulatory Changes (2024–2026)

### 10.1 Confirmed Changes

1. **Import Permit Validity Extension (2024–2026):** The permit validity period, previously a fixed 30 days, has been updated in some guidance to **30–90 days** depending on the source country. As of December 2024, New Zealand MPI guidance removed the "set validity period" stipulation, deferring to MOCCAE's current terms [^8]. UK-specific guidance in 2026 states **90 days** [^9].
2. **MOCCAE Administrative Decision 2024:** Updated high-risk country lists and accredited laboratory lists for rabies serology [^3].
3. **Dubai Municipality Digital Registration (2024–2025):** Pet registration is now fully digitized via the **Aleef app** and **Dubai Smart Services** portal, linked to UAE Pass [^13].
4. **USA CDC Classification:** The UAE is classified as a **low-risk country** for dog rabies by the CDC, simplifying US entry requirements (no titer test needed, just online CDC Dog Import Form) [^22].
5. **EU Travel Certificate Requirements:** Entry point checks for pets arriving from the UAE have become **more rigorous** in 2025–2026. Paperwork must be precise and consistent [^20].

### 10.2 Areas of Active Change / Uncertainty

> **🚩 Flagged Uncertainty:**
> - The **exact list of high-risk vs. low-risk countries** is maintained by MOCCAE and is subject to change. The official list should be verified at: https://www.moccae.gov.ae/en/services/export-import-services/import-permit-pets.aspx [^3].
> - **Rottweiler, Doberman, and Boxer status:** These breeds oscillate between "banned" and "restricted" depending on the source and the emirate. Dubai Municipality guidance from veterinary clinics lists them as restricted (not banned), but some federal-level sources include them in the banned list [^1][^13].
> - **Etihad in-cabin policy:** Etihad expanded its pet-in-cabin policy in 2024–2025, making it the most pet-friendly UAE carrier. Policies are subject to route and aircraft limitations [^17][^18].

## 11. Dubai Municipality Post-Arrival Requirements

### 11.1 Mandatory Registration

All dogs and cats must be **registered annually** with Dubai Municipality [^13].

- **Who can register:** Only UAE residents with a valid **Emirates ID** [^13].
- **Where:** Approved veterinary clinics, Dubai Municipality Veterinary Services, or online via the **Aleef app / Dubai Smart Services** [^13].
- **Requirements for registration:**
  - ISO microchip
  - Up-to-date vaccinations (rabies + core)
  - Owner's Emirates ID
  - Pet photo
  - Makani number (address) or full residence address
- **Fees:**
  - Registration: ~AED 10
  - Microchipping: ~AED 50 (at government centres; private clinics charge more)
  - Innovation fee + 5% VAT applies to services above AED 50 [^13]
- **Tag:** A physical municipality ID tag must be worn on the pet's collar at all times. A new tag is issued annually with vaccination renewal [^13].

### 11.2 Public Conduct Rules

- **Dogs must be on a lead** and **muzzled** in public places, parks, and building corridors [^13].
- **Dogs are NOT allowed** in public parks, on beaches, in shopping malls, or on public transport (except certified service animals) [^13].
- **Failure to clean up after your dog:** Fine of AED 400; repeated violations may lead to confiscation [^13].
- **Dog off-leash or unmuzzled:** First fine AED 200, second fine AED 400, confiscation for repeated violations within a year [^13].

### 11.3 Lost Pets

- If Dubai Municipality catches a stray with an ID tag: owner pays **AED 500** fine and retrieves the pet within 3 working days [^13].
- If caught **without** an ID tag: the animal may be **euthanized after 8 days** if not claimed [^13].
- **Unregistered dogs** may be released upon proof of ownership + AED 500 fine, but must then be registered and vaccinated [^13].

### 11.4 Housing & Community Rules

- Building-level pet rules vary. Some buildings allow pets, some don't. Always confirm in writing before signing a lease.
- Some buildings have size restrictions (e.g., "small pets only under 15kg").
- Gated communities (Arabian Ranches, The Springs, etc.) are generally more pet-friendly [^22].
- Pet deposits: AED 1,000–5,000 in some buildings [^22].
