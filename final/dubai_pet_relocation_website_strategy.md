### Problem / Pain Section

#### H2: Here's What Most Pet Owners Worry About

Relocating a pet is not like shipping a package. Your dog or cat is a member of your family. And the process is genuinely complex. We don't hide that. We name it, then we solve it.

#### H3: "Will My Pet Be Safe During the Flight?"

Most owners worry about the cargo hold. They picture darkness, heat, and stress. The reality is different. Pet cargo holds are climate-controlled and pressurized, just like the passenger cabin. We only book morning flights to avoid ground heat. We use IATA-certified crates with proper ventilation, door locks, and live animal labels. And we never book a flight until we have confirmed the airline's pet-specific rules in writing — not with the regular reservation desk, but with the cargo department directly.

#### H3: "What If I'm Hit With Hidden Fees?"

This is the number one complaint in the pet relocation industry. A quote starts at AED 8,000. Then the crate is extra. Then the vet visit is extra. Then the permit fee is extra. Then the boarding fee is extra. By the end, you're paying AED 15,000 and you didn't see it coming.

Our quotes are itemized and inclusive. We show you exactly what you pay, what it covers, and what costs extra — before you book. No surprises. No "oh, we forgot to mention that."

#### H3: "What If the Paperwork Is Wrong?"

One wrong document can delay your pet by weeks. A microchip number mismatch. A vaccination date that is one day off. A health certificate issued outside the 10-day window. These are real mistakes that happen every day.

We check every document three times. We submit MOCCAE permits daily. We know the current forms, the right office, and the common rejection reasons. If there's an issue, we fix it before it becomes a problem — not after your pet is stuck at customs.

### FAQ Section

#### H2: Common Questions About Pet Relocation

#### Do dogs need to be quarantined in Dubai?

No. If all documentation is complete and correct, pets do not require mandatory quarantine in Dubai. This includes a valid MOCCAE import permit, ISO microchip, rabies vaccination, and health certificate. However, incomplete documentation can result in conditional quarantine at the owner's expense. We check every document three times to prevent this.

#### How much does pet relocation to Dubai cost?

Pet relocation to Dubai typically costs between AED 8,000 and AED 25,000 depending on the origin country, pet size, and service level. Our transparent cost breakdown includes government fees (AED 200–700), veterinary costs (AED 1,200–2,500), cargo shipping (AED 3,000–12,000), and our service fee (AED 1,500–6,000). You get an estimated range in 60 seconds using our online calculator.

#### How long does pet relocation to Dubai take?

From low-risk countries like the UK or EU, the process takes 4–6 weeks. From high-risk countries requiring a rabies titer test, the minimum timeline is 4 months (including the 90-day waiting period after the titer test). We recommend starting at least 8 weeks before your planned move. If you are on a tighter timeline, message us on WhatsApp — we may have options.

#### Which dog breeds are banned in Dubai?

The UAE bans Pit Bull types (including American Bully and Staffordshire Bull Terrier), Mastiff types (including Cane Corso, Boerboel, and Bullmastiff), Japanese Tosa, and wolf-dog hybrids. Some breeds like Rottweiler and Doberman are restricted rather than fully banned. Brachycephalic breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs, Persian cats) face airline restrictions during summer months. We verify your breed's eligibility before quoting.

#### Can I get an instant quote for pet relocation?

Yes. Our online cost calculator provides an estimated range in under 60 seconds. Simply enter your pet's size, origin country, and preferred service level. For a firm, itemized quote, complete the booking form or message us on WhatsApp. We respond within 15 minutes during business hours.

## H2: Why Pet Relocation Costs Vary So Much

### H3: Four Factors That Determine Your Final Price

No two pet moves cost the same. Here's why.

**1. Your pet's size**

Cargo fees are based on the crate's **volumetric weight** — not how much your pet actually weighs. A 40kg Labrador in a large crate costs significantly more than a 5kg cat in a small one. The crate itself costs more too.

**2. Your origin country**

From the UK or EU? No rabies titer test needed. From India, Pakistan, or the Philippines? You'll need a titer test, a 90-day wait, and often more veterinary prep. That adds AED 1,500–2,500.

**3. Your travel season**

Summer is complicated. From June to September, many airlines restrict or ban pet cargo due to heat. Brachycephalic breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs, Persian cats) face additional airline bans. Off-season rerouting, ground transport, or extended boarding can add AED 2,000–5,000.

**4. Your pet's documentation status**

Already microchipped and vaccinated? You'll save AED 300–600. Starting from scratch? Budget for the full veterinary timeline. Missing one document can trigger a quarantine that costs AED 8,500+.

### H3: Why is cargo so expensive compared to my own flight ticket?

Pets travel as **manifest cargo**, not as baggage. This means they ride in a climate-controlled, pressurized section of the cargo hold — not in the passenger cabin. The fee covers:

- Dedicated cargo space (your pet's crate displaces freight that could have been sold)
- Climate control and pressurization throughout the flight
- Ground handling at both airports (check-in, loading, unloading, customs)
- Insurance and liability coverage for live animals
- IATA-compliant handling procedures

A pet's cargo ticket costs more than your economy seat because it requires specialized infrastructure, staff, and safety protocols. The good news: it's the safest way for pets to fly long distances.

### H3: Can I get a cheaper quote from a general moving company?

- MOCCAE permit fees ("that's a government fee, not our problem")

- Customs clearance at destination ("you'll need to collect your pet from the airport")

By the time you add the missing pieces, the total usually exceeds our all-inclusive quote. Plus, a mover's priority is your furniture. Our priority is your pet. Every time.

## H2: Step-by-Step: How to Import Your Pet to Dubai

### Step 1: Check Your Breed and Country (Day 0)

Before you do anything, confirm two things:

1. **Is your dog's breed banned in the UAE?** (See the banned breeds section below.) If your dog is on the list, you cannot import it — unless it is a certified service animal.
2. **Is your origin country high-risk or low-risk for rabies?** This determines whether you need a titer test and how long you'll wait.

> 🚩 **Flagged uncertainty:** The exact high-risk/low-risk country list changes. Always verify your country's status on the [MOCCAE portal](https://www.moccae.gov.ae/en/services/export-import-services/import-permit-pets.aspx) before starting.

### Step 2: Implant the Microchip (Day 0)

Your pet must have an **ISO 11784/11785 compliant 15-digit microchip**. This must be implanted **before** the rabies vaccination. If your pet was vaccinated before being microchipped, the vaccination is invalid for UAE entry.

- Cost: AED 300–600 at your local vet
- The microchip number must appear on every subsequent document

### Step 3: Rabies Vaccination (Day 0)

Your pet must be at least **12 weeks old** at the time of vaccination. The vaccine must be:

- Inactivated or recombinant (**not** attenuated live virus)
- Administered **≥21 days before travel**
- Within **12 months of arrival** (or a valid booster documented)

### Step 4: Core Vaccinations (Day 0–14)

**Dogs:** DHPPIL (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza, Leptospirosis)
**Cats:** FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia)

These are typically administered alongside the rabies vaccine or shortly after.

### Step 5: Rabies Titer Test — If Required (Day 21–31)

If your pet is from a **high-risk country**, a blood sample must be drawn **≥21 days after** the rabies vaccination. The sample is sent to a **WOAH/ISO 17025 accredited laboratory**.

- Minimum result: **≥0.5 IU/ml**
- You must then wait **90 days from the date of blood sample collection** before your pet can enter the UAE

> ⚠️ **Special exception:** Pets from **Bahrain and Qatar** must have a titer test **even though they are classified as low-risk**. This is frequently missed and causes delays.

### Step 6: Apply for MOCCAE Import Permit (Day 21–90, depending on country)

Apply online via the [MOCCAE portal](https://www.moccae.gov.ae) or the MOCCAE mobile app.

- Fee: **~AED 200 per animal**
- Validity: **30–90 days** from issue (some sources say 30 days, others 90 for UK/EU origins — verify in the portal at the time of application.)
- Processing time: **3–7 working days**
- Required: Copy of owner's passport, UAE visa, pet's microchip number, vaccination records, and titer test result (if applicable)

> 🚩 **Flagged uncertainty:** Import permit validity has changed multiple times. Apply as close to your travel date as safely possible while leaving time for flight booking.

### Step 7: Book Manifest Cargo Flight (2–4 weeks before travel)

All pets entering Dubai **must travel as manifest cargo**. They cannot fly in the cabin or as checked baggage on flights **to** Dubai.

- **Emirates SkyCargo:** Most common for DXB arrivals. 72 hours advance notice required.
- **Etihad Cargo:** Alternative for Abu Dhabi arrivals. Etihad also allows small pets in cabin **to Abu Dhabi only** (≤8 kg, $399 per segment).
- **flydubai Cargo:** Limited published pet policies — contact directly.

Cargo costs typically range from **AED 3,000–12,000** depending on route, airline, and pet size.

### Step 8: Health Certificate and Antiparasitics (Within 10–14 days of travel)

- **Antiparasitic treatment:** Internal (deworming/Praziquantel) and external (Fipronil or Permethrin) within **14 days before arrival**
- **International Health Certificate:** Issued by a government-accredited veterinarian within **10 days of arrival**

### Step 9: Arrival and Customs Clearance (Day of arrival)

Your pet arrives at DXB or DWC cargo terminal. You (or your agent) present:

- MOCCAE import permit
- Original health certificate
- Vaccination records
- Microchip certificate
- Titer test result (if applicable)
- Antiparasitic treatment record
- Owner's passport and UAE visa

If all documents are correct, customs clearance takes **2–4 hours**. Your pet does not enter quarantine.

### Step 10: Dubai Municipality Registration (Within 30 days)

Register your pet via the **Aleef app** or Dubai Smart Services portal. You need:

- Your Emirates ID
- Pet's microchip number
- Current vaccination records
- Pet photo
- Your residence address (Makani number)

- Fee: ~AED 10
- Annual renewal required

A physical municipality ID tag must be worn on your pet's collar at all times.

## H2: Import Permit Application

The MOCCAE import permit is the single most important document for bringing your pet to Dubai. Without it, your pet cannot enter the country.

### H3: How to Apply

3. **Through a licensed agent:** PetClear can submit the application on your behalf

### H3: What You'll Need

- Copy of your passport
- UAE visa or proof of residency
- Pet's ISO microchip number
- Rabies vaccination certificate (with microchip number, batch number, manufacturer, date, vet stamp)
- Core vaccination records
- Titer test result (if applicable)
- Recent photo of your pet

### H3: Fees and Timeline

- **Fee:** ~AED 200 per animal
- **Processing:** 3–7 working days

### H3: Common Application Mistakes

- **Microchip mismatch:** The microchip number on the permit application doesn't match the vaccination certificate
- **Expired vaccine:** Rabies vaccine is older than 12 months or less than 21 days old
- **Missing titer test:** Applying for a permit from a high-risk country without the titer test result
- **Wrong validity window:** Applying too early and having the permit expire before travel

## H2: Health Certificate Timing

The International Health Certificate is the last document you obtain before travel. Get the timing wrong, and it expires before your pet arrives.

- **Valid for 10 days** from the date of issuance to the date of arrival in the UAE

## H2: What Happens If Your Documents Are Wrong

This is the section most pet owners skip — and the one that causes the most pain.

### H3: No Quarantine (If Documents Are Correct)

If all your paperwork is complete, correct, and consistent, your pet **does not need to enter quarantine**. This is one of the UAE's biggest advantages as a pet relocation destination.

### H3: Conditional Quarantine (If Documents Are Wrong)

Quarantine is imposed if:
- Your pet arrives from a **high-risk country without a titer test**
- Documentation is incomplete, incorrect, or non-sequential (e.g., vaccination before microchip)
- The pet shows clinical signs of illness on arrival
- Breed identification is disputed by the inspector

**Quarantine details:**
- Location: Dubai Cargo Village or Abu Dhabi Cargo Terminal
- Cost: **Entirely borne by the owner**
- Duration: Until the issue is resolved (typically 30 days for titer testing)
- One reported case cost **AED 8,500** for a 30-day quarantine after a missing titer test

### H3: Return or Euthanasia (Worst Cases)

In extreme cases:
- The animal may be **returned to the origin country on the next available flight** at the owner's expense
- In the worst cases, **euthanasia** may be ordered if the animal poses a rabies risk and cannot be returned

> ⚠️ **Critical Warning:** These are not theoretical risks. They happen when pet owners assume "it will be fine" or rely on outdated information. We check every document three times because we've seen what happens when one date is wrong.

### H3: The Most Common Document Errors

1. **Microchip before vaccination** — Vaccination given before microchip = invalid
2. **Titer test timing** — Blood drawn before 21 days post-vaccination = invalid
3. **Permit validity** — Permit expired before travel = entry denied
4. **Health certificate expiry** — Certificate older than 10 days on arrival = quarantine risk
5. **Mismatched microchip numbers** — Different numbers on different documents = automatic rejection

## H2: Frequently Asked Questions

### H3: Do dogs need to be quarantined in Dubai?

**No.** If all documentation is complete and correct — including the MOCCAE import permit, ISO microchip, rabies vaccination, and health certificate — pets do not require mandatory quarantine in Dubai. However, incomplete documentation can result in conditional quarantine at the owner's expense.

### H3: What is the MOCCAE import permit for pets?

The MOCCAE import permit is a mandatory approval from the UAE Ministry of Climate Change and Environment required for all pets entering the UAE. It costs approximately AED 200 per animal and is valid for 30–90 days depending on the origin country. You must apply online via the MOCCAE portal or app before your pet travels.

### H3: Is a rabies titer test required for Dubai?

A rabies titer test is only required for pets coming from **high-risk rabies countries**. It is **not required** for pets from low-risk countries including the UK, EU, USA, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand. However, pets from **Bahrain and Qatar** are a special exception — they do require a titer test despite being classified as low-risk.

### H3: What documents are needed to bring a cat to the UAE?

To bring a cat to the UAE, you need: (1) MOCCAE import permit, (2) ISO 11784/11785 microchip certificate, (3) rabies vaccination certificate (≥21 days old), (4) FVRCP vaccination records, (5) international health certificate (within 10 days of travel), (6) antiparasitic treatment record, and (7) copy of owner's passport and UAE visa.

### H3: How long does pet relocation to Dubai take?

From low-risk countries like the UK or EU, the process takes **4–6 weeks**. From high-risk countries requiring a rabies titer test, the minimum timeline is **4 months** (including the 90-day waiting period after the titer test). We recommend starting the process at least **8 weeks** before your planned move.

#### H3: Step 1: Breed check and route planning

You send us your dog's breed, weight, and origin country on WhatsApp. Within 15 minutes, we confirm:

We track every deadline. We remind you when the next step is due. We check every document three times before submission.

### H2: What Your Dog Needs to Enter Dubai

These are not optional. Missing one document can result in quarantine, return to origin, or — in extreme cases — euthanasia. We do not say this to frighten you. We say it because we have seen what happens when owners use outdated checklists from the internet.

#### H3: The five documents every dog needs

1. **MOCCAE Import Permit** — Applied online via the MOCCAE portal. Valid 30–90 days. Cost: ~AED 200.

### H2: IATA Travel Crates for Dogs — Size, Ventilation, and Comfort

Your dog's crate is their home for the flight. It must be the right size, the right material, and the right ventilation. A non-compliant crate will be refused at cargo check-in — and you will miss your flight.

#### H3: How to measure your dog for the right crate

Use these three measurements:

1. **Length (A):** From tip of nose to base of tail, while standing
2. **Height (B):** From ground to top of head or ears (whichever is higher), while standing
3. **Width (C):** At the widest point of the body

**Crate minimum internal dimensions:**
- Length = A + ½B (your dog must be able to lie down with legs extended)
- Height = B + 5 cm (your dog must be able to stand without ears touching the roof)
- Width = C × 2 (your dog must be able to turn around comfortably)

#### H3: Crate sizes by dog weight and breed

| Dog Size | Weight Range | Typical Crate Size | Example Breeds | Est. Cost (AED) |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Small | Under 10 kg | 48 × 32 × 32 cm (Series 200) | Dachshund, French Bulldog, Shih Tzu | 500–700 |
| Medium | 10–25 kg | 68 × 53 × 51 cm (Series 300) | Beagle, Border Collie, Cocker Spaniel | 700–1,000 |
| Large | 25–40 kg | 81 × 61 × 61 cm (Series 400) | Labrador, Golden Retriever, German Shepherd | 1,000–1,500 |
| Extra-Large | Over 40 kg | 102 × 70 × 76 cm (Series 500/700) | Great Dane, Mastiff, Rottweiler | 1,500–2,000 |

**Important:**
- The crate must be rigid plastic or wood with a solid metal door. Wire crates are not accepted.
- Ventilation must be present on all four sides (for international flights).
- Food and water bowls must be attached to the inside of the door, accessible from outside without opening the door.
- The crate must have "Live Animal" and "This Way Up" labels on all sides.

### H2: Summer Heat Embargoes and Brachycephalic Dogs

#### H3: When dogs cannot fly (June–September)

Most airlines suspend or restrict live animal cargo when ground temperatures exceed 29.5°C (85°F). In Dubai, this means:

- **Standard embargo:** May to September (varies by airline and route)
- **Peak restriction:** June–August — most airlines will not accept dogs at all
- **Morning flights:** Even outside embargo, we book morning departures and arrivals to avoid midday heat

If your move falls during summer, your options are:
- Delay the dog's travel until October (most common)
- Ground transport to a cooler departure city, then fly
- Private pet charter (expensive, but available)
- Pet nanny escort service (limited routes)

We tell you this in your first conversation. We do not take bookings we cannot fulfil.

#### H3: ⚠️ Special warning for snub-nose breeds

**Brachycephalic (snub-nose) dogs are at higher risk during air travel.** Their shortened airways make breathing harder in heat, stress, and low humidity.

**Breeds in this category:**
- English Bulldog, French Bulldog, American Bulldog
- Pug, Pekingese, Shih Tzu, Lhasa Apso
- Boston Terrier, Boxer (some airlines)
- Chow Chow, Shar Pei
- King Charles Spaniel (some airlines)
- Japanese Chin, Brussels Griffon

**What this means for your dog:**
- Emirates restricts these breeds to **November–April only**
- Some airlines require a larger crate (10% bigger than standard)
- A veterinary fitness certificate may be required in addition to standard documents
- We strongly recommend direct flights with no transit for brachycephalic dogs

> **⚠️ PetClear Warning:** We will not book a brachycephalic dog during summer months. The risk is too high, and no airline will accept them. If your move is urgent, we will discuss ground transport or alternative routing. Your dog's safety comes before our revenue.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions About Dog Relocation to Dubai

#### Q1: How long does it take to relocate a dog to Dubai?

From low-risk countries (UK, EU, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand), the minimum timeline is **4–6 weeks**. This includes microchip, rabies vaccination (21-day wait), MOCCAE permit application, and health certificate.

From high-risk countries, the minimum timeline is **4 months** due to the rabies titer test and 90-day waiting period. We recommend starting 8–12 weeks before your planned move.

#### Q2: Will my dog be quarantined in Dubai?

**No — if all documentation is complete and correct.** The UAE does not have mandatory quarantine for compliant dogs. However, if your documents are incomplete, incorrect, or your dog is missing the titer test from a high-risk country, quarantine will be imposed at your expense. One case we know of cost AED 8,500 for 30 days. This is why we check every document three times.

#### Q3: Can I fly with my dog in the cabin to Dubai?

**No.** Regular dogs cannot travel in the cabin on flights *to* Dubai. All dogs must travel as manifest cargo. The only exception is Etihad Airways, which allows small dogs (≤8 kg) in the cabin on flights to **Abu Dhabi** (not Dubai). If you choose this option, you will need to drive from Abu Dhabi to Dubai after arrival.

#### Q4: What if my dog is a banned breed but I have paperwork saying otherwise?

Breed identification at UAE customs is done by visual inspection. If your dog looks like a banned breed, the inspector's decision is final. "Pit Bull mix" or "unknown breed" labels from shelters are not reliable for UAE import. We assess your dog's appearance, paperwork, and pedigree (if available) before we submit any permit application. If there is any doubt, we will tell you honestly.

#### Q5: Do you handle the dog's arrival at Dubai Airport, or do I need to go to the cargo terminal myself?

We handle both options. Most clients choose our **door-to-door service**: we collect your dog from the cargo terminal, clear customs, and deliver to your home. If you prefer to collect your dog yourself, we prepare all the customs paperwork and meet you at the terminal with everything ready. Either way, you get WhatsApp updates and photos at every checkpoint.

## 5. CAT-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

### H2: What Documents Does Your Cat Need to Enter Dubai?

Bringing a cat to Dubai requires specific paperwork. The rules are the same for all cats, regardless of breed or origin. Here's exactly what you need:

#### H3: 1. MOCCAE Import Permit
A mandatory approval from the UAE Ministry of Climate Change and Environment. Cost: approximately AED 200. Valid: 30–90 days. We apply for this on your behalf.

#### H3: 2. ISO Microchip (15-Digit)
Your cat must have an ISO 11784/11785 compliant microchip. This must be implanted *before* the rabies vaccination, and the microchip number must appear on every certificate. This is the most common paperwork error — and it's easily avoided with a single checklist.

#### H3: 3. Rabies Vaccination
Administered at or after 12 weeks of age. The vaccination must be at least 21 days old before your cat travels, and within 12 months of arrival. Only inactivated or recombinant vaccines are accepted — not live attenuated vaccines.

#### H3: 4. FVRCP (Core Feline Vaccines)
Your cat needs up-to-date core vaccinations: Feline Panleukopenia (FPV), Feline Rhinotracheitis (FHV-1), and Feline Calicivirus (FCV). Feline Leukemia (FeLV) is also recommended by most veterinary authorities.

#### H3: 5. International Health Certificate
Issued by an official or government-accredited veterinarian in your country of origin. This must be issued within 10 days of arrival in Dubai. It must include antiparasitic treatment records and show the same microchip number as all other documents.

#### H3: 6. Antiparasitic Treatment
Internal deworming (Praziquantel) and external treatment (Fipronil or equivalent) must be administered within 14 days of arrival. We coordinate this with our vet partner so the timing is exact.

#### H3: 7. Copy of Owner's Passport & UAE Visa
Required for the MOCCAE permit application and customs clearance. If you're not yet in Dubai, we can arrange for a representative to handle collection.

**Good news for UK, EU, USA, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand owners:** Your country is classified as low-risk for rabies. This means **no rabies titer test (RNATT) is required**, and the minimum timeline is just 4–6 weeks from start to finish. For high-risk countries, a titer test is required with a 90-day waiting period — so the timeline extends to 4 months minimum.

**Important:** Cats must be at least 4 months old to enter Dubai. If your kitten is younger, you'll need to wait or arrange temporary care until they reach the minimum age.

## 7. CRATE REQUIREMENTS

### H2: Choosing the Right Travel Crate for Your Cat

Your cat's crate is their home for the journey. It needs to be safe, comfortable, and compliant. Here's exactly what that means.

#### H3: IATA Standards for Cat Crates
All cats entering Dubai must travel in an IATA-compliant crate. This means:
- **Rigid construction:** Hard plastic or plywood with a solid roof and floor. No soft-sided carriers for cargo.
- **Secure door:** Metal door with a locking mechanism that cannot be opened from the inside. The door must close firmly without gaps.
- **Ventilation:** Openings on all four sides, covering at least 16% of the total surface area. For brachycephalic (flat-faced) cats like Persians or Exotic Shorthairs, the crate must be 10% larger than standard with increased ventilation.
- **No wheels:** Wheels must be removed or locked.
- **Leak-proof floor:** A solid, absorbent base that won't let moisture escape.
- **No top-opening lids:** Cargo crates must open from the front only.
- **Proper sizing:** Your cat must be able to stand upright without touching the roof, turn around easily, and lie down in a natural position. We measure your cat precisely — length from nose to tail base, plus height from floor to top of head — to ensure the right fit.

#### H3: Making the Crate Feel Like Home
A few simple additions make a big difference for cats:
- **A small, familiar blanket or item of your clothing:** Your scent is comforting.
- **Absorbent bedding:** A thin layer that won't bunch up. We provide vet-approved absorbent pads.
- **Spill-proof water container:** Attached to the door, filled at check-in. We freeze water in the container for long flights so it melts gradually.
- **Food tray:** Attached to the inside of the door with a small portion of dry food. We include written feeding instructions.

#### H3: What About a Litter Box?
For long-haul flights (8+ hours), some owners ask if a litter box should be included. The honest answer: most cats will not use a litter box during a flight, even if one is provided. They're more likely to hold it. For very long journeys with connections, we can arrange a comfort stop at a transit hub where your cat is taken out of the crate, offered food and water, and given a clean pad. We discuss this with you before booking.

## 9. SEDATION AND CALMING

### H2: Should You Sedate Your Cat for Flying? (Honest Guidance)

This is the question that keeps cat owners up at night. And we have a clear, honest answer.

**Most airlines and most veterinarians do not recommend sedating cats for air travel.**

Here's why: Sedation affects a cat's ability to regulate their body temperature, balance, and breathing. At altitude, these effects are amplified. A sedated cat cannot adjust their position if they become uncomfortable, cannot respond to temperature changes, and may experience respiratory depression. The risk of adverse effects is real — and most airlines explicitly refuse to transport sedated animals.

#### H3: What We Recommend Instead
A calm cat is a prepared cat. We help you reduce travel stress without medication:
- **Crate familiarity:** Start crate training 2–3 weeks before travel. Feed your cat in the crate. Let them nap in it. Make it a safe space, not a signal that something scary is happening.
- **Feliway or calming pheromones:** We recommend spraying the crate interior with a feline pheromone spray 30 minutes before departure. This is non-medicinal and widely used by vets.
- **Timing:** We book morning flights. Cats are naturally more active at dawn and dusk; a morning departure aligns with their rhythm.
- **Direct routing:** We avoid unnecessary connections. The fewer transitions, the less stress.
- **Hunger, not fasting:** We do not recommend fasting cats before travel. A small meal 4–6 hours before departure is fine. Water should be available until check-in.

#### H3: When Medication Might Be Appropriate
There are rare cases — extreme anxiety, medical conditions — where a vet may prescribe a mild anti-anxiety medication. If this is necessary, we coordinate with our vet partner to choose the safest option, at the lowest effective dose, and we inform the airline in writing. We do not recommend this as a default. We recommend it only when a trusted vet says it's necessary.

The bottom line: Most cats travel calmly without sedation. Preparation beats medication.

## 10. SEASONAL CONSIDERATIONS

### H2: Summer Heat, Long-Haired Cats, and Seasonal Planning

#### H3: Summer Heat Embargo (June–September)
From June through September, most airlines impose heat embargoes on pet cargo. This means pets cannot be transported if the ground temperature at any point along the route exceeds 29.5°C (85°F). For Dubai, this effectively means:
- **June–August:** Very limited cargo options. Some routes are suspended entirely.
- **May and September:** Restricted — some routes available, some not.
- **October–April:** Normal operations.

**What this means for your cat:** If your move falls during summer, you have options. We can arrange:
- Early morning or late evening flights on cooler days (when available)
- Ground transport to a cooler origin hub for departure
- Boarding with our vet partner until the embargo lifts
- A pet travel nanny service (in-cabin escort on a passenger route, where permitted)

We will always tell you honestly if your timeline is realistic. We never book a flight we wouldn't put our own cat on.

#### H3: Long-Haired and Flat-Faced Cats
Persian cats, Himalayan cats, and other long-haired or brachycephalic breeds need extra care in warm weather. Their shorter nasal passages and thicker coats make them more sensitive to heat. We take this into account when choosing:
- Flight times (morning departures only)
- Crate ventilation (increased airflow for brachycephalic cats)
- Route selection (direct flights, minimal ground time)
- Seasonal timing (if possible, we recommend October–April for these breeds)

If your cat is a Persian, Exotic Shorthair, or similar breed, we'll discuss these considerations with you before booking anything.
